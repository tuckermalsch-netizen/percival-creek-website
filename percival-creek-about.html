Our Story

More Than a
Beverage Company


Percival Creek is an undeniably worthy expression of the waters, flavors, and history of the Pacific Northwest's Tumwater Craft District.


We didn't set out to just make good beer. We set out to prove that craft education, community investment, and exceptional quality could exist in the same bottle — and that every pour could mean something beyond the glass.

"
The money we earn through the sale of Percival Creek products is reinvested into the program and its amazing students. Thank you for your support — and enjoy.
Percival Creek · Tumwater, WA
01

The Waters We're Named For

Percival Creek winds through the heart of Tumwater, Washington — carrying the spirit of a region shaped by clean water, fertile land, and generations of makers. We took our name from these waters because they represent what we stand for: something rooted, something real, and something worth preserving.

The Tumwater Craft District, where our tasting room and production facilities live, sits within a mile of the historic Olympia Brewery — one of the most iconic brewing sites in the Pacific Northwest. We're aware of our place in that lineage, and we take it seriously.

02

A Collaboration Unlike Any Other

Percival Creek is produced in partnership with South Puget Sound Community College's nationally recognized Craft Brewing & Distilling program. Our students don't just study the craft — they live it. Every batch of beer, every cider pressing, every spirit run is led by students gaining real-world experience under professional guidance.

The result? Products that surprise people. Products that win over skeptics. Products made with the kind of care and enthusiasm you can actually taste.

03

Every Purchase Does More

When you buy Percival Creek, the money doesn't disappear into a corporate bottom line. It flows directly back into the SPSCC Craft Brewing & Distilling program — funding equipment, scholarships, and opportunities for students who might not otherwise have access to careers in this industry.

So raise a glass. You're not just enjoying a great drink — you're helping build the next great generation of craft makers.

"It starts with the ingredients."
